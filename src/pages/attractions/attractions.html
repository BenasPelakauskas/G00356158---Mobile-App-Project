Attractions

Attraction include:

St. Kilians Church and Hertiage Center

St.Kilians Church was built in the late 1850's with Victorian Neo-Gothic design.

The Heritage Center features a theatre room, cafe and a museum
showing historicl artefacts from the area.

Mullagh Lake

Mullagh lake is a lake approximately 1km from the town.
There is a 7km walking route around the lake which is popular with locals.
The lake is popular with fishing and many people swim in the summer.
It features a newly built jetti and a small slipway for boat launching.
Beside the lake, is Mullagh Hill, with a peak of 250m above sea level,
there is a path to the top with picnic tables.
On the far side of the peak facing the town, there is a cross and view where you
can see the town, Kells, Virginia, and on a clear day even the Wicklow and Mourne Mountains.

Teampall Cheallaigh

Teampall Cheallaigh are the ruins of the old church, currently on the land of
the Church of Ireland. There is also a graveyard nearby dedicated to local famous people.

The Park

The park is located near the town center, with two pubs nearby, it is popular for recreation.

Mullagh Fair

Every year, Mullagh fair is hosted in the town where farmers come from all around
the country to buy and sell livestock. There are also many fun events.
Last year, the world record was broken for the most people named "Cillian" in one area.
During this event, the whole town is closed and the main street is filled with animals
and stalls.
